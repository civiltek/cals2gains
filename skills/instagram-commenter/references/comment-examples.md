## Category A — Nutritional Data

Share a specific, verifiable fact with numbers. This is the highest-value category.

### Spanish

> Dato curioso: 100g de garbanzos cocidos tienen ~19g de proteina y un perfil de
> aminoacidos bastante completo para ser legumbre, solo les falta un poco de metionina.
> Combinados con arroz se complementan perfecto

> Lo que mucha gente no sabe es que el aguacate tiene mas potasio que el platano —
> 485mg vs 358mg por 100g. Y encima aporta grasas monoinsaturadas que ayudan a
> absorber vitaminas liposolubles

> El tema de la ventana anabolica esta bastante sobreestimado. La sintesis proteica se
> mantiene elevada hasta 24-48h post-entreno segun varios estudios recientes. Lo
> importante es el total diario, no si tomas el batido 30 min despues

### English

> Fun fact: Greek yogurt has roughly 10g of protein per 100g vs regular yogurt's 3-4g,
> and most of that difference comes from the straining process concentrating the casein.
> Plus you get way more satiety per calorie

> What most people miss about sweet potatoes vs white potatoes — the glycemic index
> difference almost disappears when you eat them with protein and fat. A baked sweet
> potato alone is GI ~70, not that different from white at ~78

> The bioavailability difference between plant and animal iron is massive — heme iron
> absorption is 15-35% while non-heme sits at 2-20%. Adding vitamin C to a plant-based
> meal can boost non-heme absorption by up to 6x though

## Category B — Personal Experience

Frame nutritional knowledge as lived experience. Relatable and human.

### Spanish

> Yo antes desayunaba cereales pensando que era sano hasta que me di cuenta que eran
> 45g de azucar por racion. Cambie a avena con frutos secos y la diferencia en energia
> durante la manana fue brutal

> Llevo 3 meses haciendo meal prep los domingos y ha sido un cambio total. No solo
> como mejor sino que gasto menos. El truco es cocinar proteinas y carbos por separado
> y combinar durante la semana

> Empece a trackear mi ingesta de fibra y resulta que no llegaba ni a 15g diarios
> cuando lo recomendado son 25-30g. Desde que meto mas legumbres y verdura la
> digestion ha mejorado muchisimo

### English

> I used to think all calories were equal until I started tracking macros seriously.
> Eating 2000 cals of mostly protein and fiber vs 2000 of processed carbs — completely
> different energy levels and hunger throughout the day

> Switched from whey concentrate to isolate because of bloating and it made a huge
> difference. Turns out I was mildly lactose intolerant and the concentrate has way
> more lactose (~5-6% vs <1%)

> Started adding creatine monohydrate about 6 months ago — 5g daily, nothing fancy.
> Strength went up noticeably within the first 3 weeks and recovery between sets
> improved. Probably the most evidence-backed supplement out there after protein

## Category C — Opinion/Debate

Take a respectful position. Invite discussion.

### Spanish

> Creo que el problema real con las dietas no es la falta de informacion sino el
> exceso. La gente esta tan perdida entre keto, ayuno intermitente, paleo que al
> final no hace nada. A veces lo simple funciona mejor — comer mas proteina, mas
> verdura y menos ultraprocesados

> Impopular opinion: los suplementos de BCAA son innecesarios si ya consumes
> suficiente proteina. Los BCAA ya estan en cualquier fuente proteica completa.
> Es tirar el dinero basicamente

> No estoy de acuerdo con demonizar los carbohidratos. El cerebro necesita ~120g
> de glucosa al dia para funcionar bien. El problema no son los carbos, es la
> calidad y la cantidad

### English

> Hot take: the obsession with "clean eating" labels does more harm than good. A
> food isn't inherently good or bad — context matters. 200g of rice after a heavy
> workout serves a completely different purpose than 200g of rice sitting on a couch

> I think people overcomplicate protein timing. Unless you're a competitive athlete
> training twice a day, total daily intake matters way more than whether you had
> your shake within 30 minutes post-workout

> Unpopular opinion: most people don't need a pre-workout supplement. A cup of
> coffee (80-100mg caffeine) plus a banana 30 min before training does essentially
> the same thing for a fraction of the cost

## Tone Guidelines

Reading these examples, notice:

- **Casual but informed** — like texting a friend who happens to know nutrition science
- **Specific numbers** — grams, percentages, milligrams make comments credible
- **No emojis** (or max 1) — keeps it from looking like marketing
- **No hashtags** — real commenters don't hashtag in comments
- **Spanish comments skip accents** — this is how most people actually type on Instagram
- **2-5 sentences** — enough to add value, short enough to actually get read
- **Never mentions Cals2Gains** — the profile bio does the marketing, not the comments
